Fylepad offers extensive customization options to make your writing environment comfortable and personal. Adjust themes, fonts, text colors, and formatting to match your preferences.

## Theme settings

Fylepad supports both light and dark themes with automatic system detection.

### Switching themes

You can change themes in several ways:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop">
    Click the theme toggle icon in the top-right corner of the screen:
    - Sun icon: Switches to light mode
    - Moon icon: Switches to dark mode
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Mobile">
    1. Tap the menu icon (three lines) in the top-right
    2. Select the theme toggle from the dropdown
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
Your theme preference is saved automatically and will persist across sessions.
</Note>

### Theme features

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Light mode" icon="sun">
    Clean, bright interface with high contrast. Perfect for well-lit environments and daytime use.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Dark mode" icon="moon">
    Easy on the eyes with reduced brightness. Ideal for low-light conditions and night-time writing.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Font customization

Choose from over 30 fonts to find the perfect typeface for your writing style.

### Changing fonts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open styling menu">
    Click the styling icon (paint palette) in the top toolbar
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a font">
    - Click the font dropdown to see all available fonts
    - Use the search bar to quickly find a specific font
    - Click on any font name to apply it
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preview in real-time">
    Font names are shown in their actual typeface, so you can preview before selecting
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Available font categories

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sans-serif fonts">
    Modern, clean fonts perfect for readability:
    - **Geist** (default) - Modern, highly readable
    - **Inter** - Designed for UI and long-form text
    - **Outfit** - Rounded, friendly appearance
    - **SpaceGrotesk** - Technical, contemporary feel
    - **Arial** - Classic, widely compatible
    - **Helvetica Now** - Updated classic design
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Serif fonts">
    Traditional fonts with character:
    - **Instrument Serif** - Elegant, modern serif
    - **Libre Baskerville** - Traditional book-style font
    - **Playfair Display** - Sophisticated, high-contrast
    - **Prata** - Delicate, refined
    - **Georgia** - Web-optimized readability
    - **Times New Roman** - Classic print font
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Monospace fonts">
    Fixed-width fonts ideal for code and technical writing:
    - **Roboto Mono** - Clean, readable monospace
    - **Consolas** - Microsoft's programming font
    - **Courier New** - Classic typewriter style
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Display & decorative fonts">
    Eye-catching fonts for titles and emphasis:
    - **Bebas Neue** - Bold, condensed uppercase
    - **Anton** - Heavy impact font
    - **Bangers** - Comic book style
    - **Pacifico** - Handwritten brush script
    - **Permanent Marker** - Casual marker style
    - **Dancing Script** - Elegant cursive
    - **Ink Free** - Casual handwriting
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Specialized fonts">
    Fonts for specific writing needs:
    - **Amiri Arabic** - Arabic script support
    - **Cairo Arabic** - Modern Arabic font
    - **Gulzar Urdu** - Urdu script support
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Resetting fonts

To return to the default font:

1. Open the styling menu
2. Click the trash icon next to the font selector
3. The font will reset to Geist (default)

<Tip>
Experiment with different fonts for different types of content. Try serif fonts for long-form writing and monospace for technical notes.
</Tip>

## Text color

Customize the color of your text for emphasis or visual organization.

### Applying text color

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open styling menu">
    Click the styling icon in the toolbar
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select text (optional)">
    Select the text you want to color, or set the color for new text
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose a color">
    - Click the color circle in the "Color" section
    - Pick any color from the color picker
    - The selected text will update immediately
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Resetting text color

Click the trash icon next to the color picker to remove custom colors and return to the default text color.

<Warning>
Custom text colors are saved within your note content. Use them thoughtfully to maintain readability in both light and dark themes.
</Warning>

## Text alignment

Align your text to create professional-looking documents.

### Available alignments

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Left align">
    Default alignment. Text aligns to the left margin (most common for Western languages).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Center align">
    Centers text on the page. Perfect for titles, headings, and poetry.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Right align">
    Aligns text to the right margin. Useful for signatures or special formatting.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Justify">
    Stretches text to align with both left and right margins. Creates a clean, professional appearance for paragraphs.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Changing alignment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open styling menu">
    Click the styling icon in the toolbar
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Place cursor">
    Click in the paragraph or heading you want to align
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select alignment">
    Click one of the four alignment icons in the "Text align" section. The active alignment is highlighted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Text alignment applies to the current paragraph or heading. Each paragraph can have its own alignment setting.
</Note>

## Customization tips

  <Card title="Match your workflow" icon="sliders">

  </Card>

  </Card>

## Styling shortcuts

<Tip>
All styling options are non-destructive. You can always reset fonts and colors to defaults without affecting your content.
</Tip>

Remember: The best customization is one that helps you focus on writing. Start with defaults and adjust only what improves your experience.
